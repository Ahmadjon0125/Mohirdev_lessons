Industrial safety, labour protection, and environmental protection briefing at the production facilities of the "Hauzak" GPS of "LUKOIL Uzbekistan Operating Company" LLC.
Brief characteristics of the location areas of the "Hauzak" GPS sites.
Gas Production Shop — "Hauzak" GPS is located in the Bukhara region of the Republic of Uzbekistan and includes the "North Shady" site, the "Kuvachi-Alat" site and the "Hauzak-West Shady" site.
The "North Shady" and "Hauzak-West Shady" sites are located in the Bukhara region of the Republic of Uzbekistan in the Alat district and are geographically part of the Dengizkul field. The sites are located in a desert zone far from populated areas on the southern and northern sides of Lake Dengizkul. The lake has the status of an ornithological reserve and is a major ornithological territory of international importance. To prevent negative impacts on Lake "Dengizkul" and preserve its biological significance, a biodiversity conservation action plan has been developed and is being implemented. Around the lake, there is a water protection zone at a distance of 500 meters from the water's edge and critical habitats for avifauna have been designated.
Climatic and physico-geographical characteristics of the sites' location.
The territory where all sites are located belongs to the first climatic region. The climate is sharply continental, characterized by low atmospheric precipitation, low winter temperatures, and fairly high summer temperatures. Relative air humidity is low. The prevailing wind direction is northerly, which during certain periods of the year turns into dust storms. The relief of the sites belongs to a plain-lowland area with a hilly surface covered by sands. Soils are highly saline and possess high corrosive activity towards metals.
"Hauzak-West Shady" site brief characteristic.
The area of the Dengizkul gas condensate field is characterized by complex geological structure, high reservoir pressure, and significant reserves of gas and condensate. The gas is characterized by a high content of hydrogen sulphide. Within the "Hauzak-West Shady" site, one disposal well and 49 production wells have been put into operation, grouped pads as well: K 1, K 2, K 9, K 11, K 12, K 14, K 16, K 19, K 242, K 301. Gas from the wells is supplied to the well pads via individual flowlines, where all flowlines are combined into a single manifold, through which gas from the well pad is delivered to the Preliminary Gas Treatment Unit (PGTU). Subsequently, treatment and separation of hydrocarbon condensate, produced water, and gas take place. After separation and filtration, the gas is supplied to the Booster Compressor Station (BCS) for compression followed by transportation to the tie-in points of the "Dengizkul - MPGP" and "Urtabulak - MPGP" main gas pipelines. Condensate is transported via a condensate pipeline to the tie-in point of the "Kokdumalak - Bukhara Oil Refinery" main condensate pipeline.

"Kuvachi-Alat" Site
The Kuvachi-Alat site is located in the Bukhara region of the Republic of Uzbekistan, partially in the Karakul and Alat districts, and is geographically part of the Kandym gas condensate field. The site is situated in the southwestern part of the Kyzylkum desert.
12–15 km south of the "Kuvachi-Alat" Site is the "Khodzha Davlet" Key Ornithological Area, through which a motor road passes, connecting the "Kuvachi-Alat" and "North Shady" Sites. The territory with an area of 4,242 ha is located between the motor road and two canals: Amukarakul and Yamandzhar. The Operator's personnel working at the work sites and the personnel of all contracting organizations are obliged to treat all environmental objects with care, prevent their destruction, pollution, or damage, and are required to strictly adhere to the information materials and signs posted on the territory of the sites. The operator's personnel must strictly observe the internal labour regulations and the Operator's internal instructions in the field of environmental protection and rational use of natural resources.
"Kuvachi-Alat" Site. Brief characteristic.
In accordance with the development project for the Kandym group of fields, 23 production wells, 1 observation well, and 1 disposal well have been put into operation within the "Kuvachi-Alat" site. Gas from the Alat group wells is supplied via individual flowlines to the PGTU; gas from the Kuvachi K1 and K2 group wells is supplied to the GSP-1 (Gas Gathering Station), where it is combined into a common manifold and supplied to the PGTU. Gas from the K3 wells is supplied to GSP-2, where it is combined in the metering and main manifold and supplied to CP-3 (Control Point) for well flow rate measurement; from CP-3, the gas is delivered to the PGTU via the main manifold.
Subsequently, natural gas treatment takes place with the separation of the fluid into gas, hydrocarbon condensate, and produced water. The gas undergoes primary treatment and is transported via a 500 mm diameter pipeline from the "Kuvachi-Alat" PGTU to the "North Shady" PGTU for further transportation to the tie-in point in the Dengizkul – Mubarek gas pipeline.

Hazards and risks at production facilities
The equipment of hazardous production facilities at the "Hauzak" gas production shop, the depressurization of which can lead to uncontrolled releases of hazardous substances, includes:
    • Vessels and pipelines with high and low-pressure gas and condensate of primary gas and condensate treatment units.
    • Components of pumping and compressor equipment.
Hazards and risks at production facilities also include:
    • Natural gas and hydrocarbon-containing liquid with hydrogen sulfide content under pressure in wells and inside technological equipment.
    • Unfavourable sanitary and epidemiological conditions, including possible bites from reptiles and insects.
    • Complex geological conditions.
The product in the production equipment is fire and explosion hazardous!
Hydrogen sulphide present in the extracted raw material—up to 4% by volume—represents the greatest danger to humans. Hydrogen sulphide is a colourless gas with the Odor of rotten eggs. It is a potent nerve agent, causing death from respiratory arrest. It has an irritating effect on the respiratory tract and eyes. It burns with a bluish flame, forming water and sulphur dioxide. The ignition temperature is 246°C. A perceptible smell of hydrogen sulphide is noted at concentrations up to 2 mg/m³; a significant smell at 4 mg/m³; a heavy smell from 7 to 11 mg/m³. At higher concentrations, the smell is less intense as olfactory fatigue (habituation) occurs. At concentrations of 200–260 mg/m³, burning in the eyes, irritation of the mucous membranes of the eyes and throat, and a metallic taste in the mouth are observed. At a concentration of 750 mg/m³, dangerous poisoning occurs within 15–20 minutes. At concentrations of 1000 mg/m³ and above, death occurs almost instantly. In mixtures with hydrocarbons, the maximum permissible concentration (MPC) of hydrogen sulphide in the air of the working zone is 3 mg/m³. The MPC of hydrogen sulfidic in the air of populated areas is 0.008 mg/m³.
Main sources of danger
The main sources of danger are the following types of work:
    • Drilling and workover of production wells
    • Performance of work on pipelines and technological equipment
    • Geophysical surveys
    • Seismic survey works
    • Construction works
    • Operation of motor vehicles and special equipment
On fire and explosion hazardous facilities, it is prohibited to:
    • Use open flames, smoke
    • Use mobile phones and other electronic devices not in explosion-proof design
    • Wear shoes with metal horseshoes or nails.
Main methods of protection against hazards and risks
The Operator takes the necessary measures to create safe working conditions at workplaces and protect workers from hazards and risks.
The Golden Rules for industrial safety, labour protection, and environmental protection complement the existing integrated management system in this area. During the work process, they are aimed at ensuring the safety of the Operator's employees and are communicated to each of them. Employees are provided with all necessary equipment for the safe performance of labour duties according to the regulations on providing personal protective equipment (PPE).
A disposable self-contained breathing apparatus is used as personal respiratory protection. While on a hazardous production facility, the continuous wearing of a personal gas detector and a self-rescuer is mandatory.
Technical characteristics of the PDU-5P self-rescuer:
    • protective action time: at least 20 minutes under average physical load (performing work corresponding to medium intensity loads or evacuation (walking))
    • at least 7 minutes under heavy physical load (performing heavy work or rapid evacuation (running))
    • at least 70 minutes in a state of relative rest (while waiting for help).
Rule for using the self-rescuer
Upon receiving the self-rescuer, you must check the condition of the case: the absence of cracks, punctures, dents, and other damage; the presence of the seal; and the presence of the shoulder and waist belt fastening system. If defects are found, replace the self-rescuer with a functional one. The self-rescuer can be worn over the shoulder, on the side behind the back, or on the belt. The belt length is adjusted using a buckle. To use the self-rescuer, the following should be done:
    • Remove the helmet
    • Position the PDU-5P to the "ready" position using the self-tightening buckles
    • Open the case by pulling the ring to tear off the coupling tape
    • Holding the mask, take out the working part by pulling its neck strap and unfold the working part
    • Take a breath and hold it
    • Put the mask on your head
    • Start the regenerative cartridge by pressing the metal cap of the starter ampoule all the way in
    • Exhale and continue breathing into the PDU-5
    • After connecting, it is necessary to tighten the mask harness straps starting from the bottom ones
    • Evidence of the correct use of the PDU-5 is the filling of the breathing bag and the inflow of a warm gas breathing mixture upon inhalation
    • You must leave the emergency zone with a fast and steady pace.
Personal gas detectors "GAS ALERT EXTREME" and "SP SECOND"
They are designed to warn of hydrogen sulphides concentrations above the established maximum permissible concentration level. They are personal safety devices. Upon receiving them, conduct an external inspection; there should be no damage to the integrity of the body or display. Gas detectors have a steel clip for fastening to outer clothing. It is not permitted to fasten the detector under a jacket.
Procedure for using the "GAS ALERT EXTREME" gas detector
    • To activate it, press the far-left button; normal operation will begin after the self-diagnostic test is completed. The other buttons are for settings; using them is strictly prohibited.
    • Upon leaving a gas-hazardous facility, deactivate the detector; to do this, press and hold the button for 5 seconds.
The following hydrogen sulphide measurement ranges are set on the detector:
2 ppm, which corresponds to 2.84 mg/m³, — the first trigger threshold. This activates the low-level alarm — a slow audible and vibration signal, and slow flashing of the ALARM label.
5 ppm, which corresponds to 7.1 mg/m³, — the second trigger threshold. This activates the high-level alarm — a fast audible and vibration signal, and fast flashing of the ALARM label.
Procedure for using the "SP SECOND" gas detector
    • To turn it on, you must press and hold the "POWER" button. After the detector is switched on, the measured gas concentration will be displayed, all display elements will light up, and the signal LED, audible alarm, and vibration signal will activate sequentially; alarm thresholds A1-2 ppm and A2-5 ppm will be shown. At each increase to alarm thresholds A1 and A2, the detector provides visual and multi-tonal audible signals. You must immediately apply the PDU-5 and leave the work zone.
    • Upon leaving a gas-hazardous facility, deactivate the detector; to do this, press and hold the "POWER" button.
Procedure for using the "HONEYWELL BW SOLO" gas detector
    • To turn it on, you must press the single control button. After pressing the button, the display backlight turns on. Press the button and hold it for 3 seconds. When the device turns on, it begins a countdown: "3, 2, 1". The LEDs light up, vibration activates, an audible signal is given, and the Honeywell logo is displayed on the screen.
    • Upon leaving a gas-hazardous facility, deactivate the detector; to do this, press and hold the button for 3 seconds while the countdown is in progress.
Procedure for using the "GAS ALERT EXTREME" gas detector
    • To activate it, press the [OK] button. Before the device turns on, a countdown "3, 2, 1" will appear on the display. All display elements will light up. Then, the signal LED, the audible alarm device, and the vibration signal will turn on sequentially.
    • Upon leaving a gas-hazardous facility, deactivate the detector; to do this, simultaneously press and hold both the [OK] and [+] buttons for approximately 2 seconds until the number "3" appears on the display. Do not release both buttons until the countdown is finished. Shutdown is confirmed by a beep signal.
The following hydrogen sulphide measurement ranges are set on the detector:
2 ppm, which corresponds to 2.84 mg/m³, — the first trigger threshold. This activates the low-level alarm — a slow audible and vibration signal, and slow flashing of the ALARM label.
5 ppm, which corresponds to 7.1 mg/m³, — the second trigger threshold. This activates the high-level alarm — a fast audible and vibration signal, and fast flashing of the ALARM label.

Evacuation in the event of fire or gas release
In accordance with the requirements of the legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, an Emergency Response Plan (ERP) has been developed at the "Hauzak" GPS. This document is mandatory for all enterprises operating gas-explosion and fire-hazardous facilities where emergency situations are possible. The Emergency Response Plan defines effective measures and actions for saving lives and liquidating an accident in the initial stage of its development. For rapid notification during accidents and incidents, the ERP provides a notification scheme for officials of the "Hauzak" GPS and interested territorial organizations regarding emergency and extraordinary situations arising at "Hauzak" GPS facilities. To determine evacuation routes, it is necessary to determine the wind direction; you must exit the gas-contaminated zone perpendicularly to the wind direction.
In the event of an emergency, the following is performed:
    • Equipment shutdown
    • Evacuation of personnel from production facilities
    • Notification of emergency services according to the notification scheme and their deployment to the accident area
    • Personnel exit to the assembly point
    • Report by site managers on the presence of subordinates
    • Evacuation of personnel to a safe place
    • Gas rescue teams entering the accident zone to conduct reconnaissance and carry out victims
    • After receiving permission to enter the territory, firefighters perform combat deployment; special emergency services proceed to the containment and elimination of accidents.
Providing first aid to victims of hydrogen sulphide poisoning
Hydrogen sulphide poisoning causes a severe illness, the outcome of which depends on the speed of action taken. The first signs of hydrogen sulphide poisoning are: redness of the eyeball and eyelids, a metallic taste in the mouth, and nausea. In case of hydrogen sulphide poisoning, it is necessary to immediately call for emergency medical assistance.
In all shift camps, there is a medical station with a duty shift doctor available 24 hours a day.
In the administrative building, the dispatcher has a medical first aid kit where you can go in case of worsening health or minor injuries.
If during your visit to production facilities you experience anxiety about anything, immediately report it to the security service or the dispatch office, as well as to the Industrial Safety and Labor Protection Service.
Rules for the use of primary fire extinguishing equipment 
Rules for using fire extinguishers:
    • bring the fire extinguisher to the fire source no closer than 3 meters, break the seal, pull the pin by the ring, point the nozzle at the source of ignition, press the lever on the body all the way, and discharge the extinguisher by pressing the lever.
PROCEDURE FOR FILLING OUT THE KNO (HAZARD OBSERVATION CARD). 
At the Operator's production facilities, a methodology for accounting and analysing near-misses (incidents without consequences) is in operation. KNO forms for completion are located on information stands near the KNO collection boxes. If dangerous actions, conditions, or near-misses are detected, the observer fills out a hazard observation card. The completed KNO form is placed in the KNO collection box. Subsequently, a representative of the facility's IS, LP, and EP (Industrial Safety, Labor Protection, and Environmental Protection) Service opens the box and processes the KNOs completed by observers, with subsequent entry into the logbook.
